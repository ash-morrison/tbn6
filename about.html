About The Bookish Network

A reader‑first, community‑powered news hub for the world of books (demo).

Our Mission

The Bookish Network exists to help readers discover great work and to help the people who make books—booksellers, librarians, publishers, authors—find each other. We aim for reporting that’s transparent, practical, and welcoming: strong sourcing, clear language, and layouts that work for everyone, including screen‑reader users and learners on mobile data.

A Brief History (Demo)

  • 2012: A small weekly email called The Bookish starts as a side project by two librarians who wanted a quick, human‑curated alternative to algorithmic book feeds.
  • 2015: The email becomes a blog and a community calendar for indie bookstore events across the Northeast. Volunteer contributors—booksellers, school media specialists, and public librarians—join in.
  • 2018: The first Bookish podcast debuts, mixing author craft talks with behind‑the‑counter stories from shops and library branches.
  • 2020: During the pandemic, the team builds a lightweight directory to help readers buy local and request titles from libraries. Traffic surges; partnerships with small presses follow.
  • 2024: The project relaunches as The Bookish Network—an umbrella for reporting, explainers, toolkits, and live programming. The focus expands to accessibility, metadata hygiene, and sustainability across the book supply chain.
  • 2026: Today’s demo site showcases a modern, accessible news layout built from plain HTML/CSS/JS—no frameworks required. Stories here are simulated for teaching and workshop use.

How We Work (Demo)

  • Clarity first: Plain‑language explanations and concise headlines.
  • Accessibility: Alt text on images, keyboard‑friendly controls, and readable color contrast.
  • Indie‑minded: Attention to libraries and independent booksellers as engines of discovery.
  • Climate‑aware: Curious about the real math: print runs, returns, freight, and materials.

This site is a demo. Names, quotes, and organizations in articles are simulated for instructional use.
